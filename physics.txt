In this work, we propose the Quantum Wasserstein Distance of order 2 ($W_2$) as a robust geometric metric for lossy quantum sensing. Unlike overlap-based measures, $W_2$ quantifies the transport cost in phase space and maintains a linear response to channel transmissivity, even in regimes where the quantum state is virtually indistinguishable from thermal noise.
