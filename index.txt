However, existing methods typically feed LLMs with tokens from multi-view and multi-frame images independently, leading to redundant computation and limited spatial consistency. This separation in visual processing hinders accurate 3D spatial reasoning and fails to maintain geometric coherence across views. On the other hand, Bird's-Eye View (BEV) representations learned from geometrically annotated tasks (e.g., object detection) provide spatial structure but lack the semantic richness of foundation vision encoders. To bridge this gap, we propose BEVLM, a framework that connects a spatially consistent and semantically distilled BEV representation with LLMs. Through extensive experiments, we show that BEVLM enables LLMs to reason more effectively in cross-view driving scenes, improving accuracy by 46%, by leveraging BEV features as unified inputs.
