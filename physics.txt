We show these problems belong to one of three complexity phases: QMA-complete, StoqMA-complete, and reducible to a new problem we call EPR*. The phases are physically interpretable, corresponding to the energy level ordering of the local term.   The EPR* problem is a simple generalization of the EPR problem of King. Inspired by empirically efficient algorithms for EPR, we conjecture that EPR* is in BPP. If true, this would complete the complexity classification of these problems, and imply EPR* is the transition point between easy and hard local Hamiltonians.
