However, existing methods typically feed LLMs with tokens from multi-view and multi-frame images independently, leading to redundant computation and limited spatial consistency. This separation in visual processing hinders accurate 3D spatial reasoning and fails to maintain geometric coherence across views. On the other hand, Bird's-Eye View (BEV) representations learned from geometrically annotated tasks (e.g., object detection) provide spatial structure but lack the semantic richness of foundation vision encoders.
